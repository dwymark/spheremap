Sphere projections

per-pixel inverse projection, Perlin noise on the sphere, blue equator to white pole

what you are looking at

Each pixel of the canvas is mapped to projection coordinates (x, y) and pushed through the selected projection's inverse to get (φ, λ) on the sphere. The (φ, λ) then drives a 3D Perlin noise field over the unit sphere and a latitude-driven color mix from blue equator to white pole.

The library is src/projections/*.js plus src/renderer/*.js. Each projection carries a JS forward/inverse and a GLSL inverse; the renderer splices the GLSL into a fragment shader at compile time. Adding a new projection means writing one file and pushing it to the registry.

source

Snyder, USGS PP 1395 (1987) — equation numbers cited per projection.
